I am a thesis-track M.S. student in Electrical Engineering at Northwestern University, advised by Prof. Randy Freeman in Northwestern's Center for Robotics and Biosystems. My research interests lie in **humanoid manipulation**, **physical human-robot interaction**, **human intent and motion prediction**, **robot learning**, and **safe autonomy**.

My current research focuses on high-level human-robot interaction for humanoid robots. I work on collaborative frameworks between humans and humanoid robots, including expectation modeling, motion prediction, simulation, deployment, and real-robot validation. I am particularly interested in building robotic systems that can infer human intent, adapt to uncertainty, and operate reliably during physical interaction.

Previously, I received my B.S. degrees in **Electrical Engineering** and **Applied Mathematics** from the University of California San Diego. My broader background includes machine learning, control systems, robotic perception, computer vision, autonomous vehicle systems, and robotics design.

## Research Interests

* Humanoid manipulation and control
* Physical human-robot interaction
* Human intent, expectation, and motion prediction
* Robot learning and shared autonomy
* Safe autonomy and reliable real-robot deployment
* Computer vision and robotic perception
